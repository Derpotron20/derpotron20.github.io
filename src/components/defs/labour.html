Labour Market:

  • Arbritration:

    • Formal dispute where two parties refer to an independant 3rd party for determination

  • Award:

    • Minimum wages and working conditions.

  • Casualisation of Work:

    • Employ increasing proportion of workforce as casual workers.

  • Conciliation:

    • Process for working through problems between two parties using a 3rd party mediator to reach an agreement.

  • Contractor:

    • Firm enters a contract at a fixed price.

  • Corporitisation:

    • Running a government enterprise like a private company.

  • Cyclical Unemployment:

    • Unemployment arising from different stages in the Business Cycle.

  • Derived Demand:

    • Demand for productive resource.

  • Employer Associations:

    • Represent employers and advance their interests.

  • Employment to Working Age Population Ratio:

    • People employed as a percentage of working age population (15-64).

  • Enterprise Bargaining:

    • Negotiation between employers and employees in terms of industrial relations.

  • Fair Work Act:

    • Employment and industrial relations procedures of Australia.

  • Frictional Unemployment:

    • Unemployment arising from are long/difficult transitions between jobs.

  • Human Capital:

    • Accumulation of skills, knowledge, and experience that an individual can use in production.

  • Industrial Dispute:

    • Disput or disagreement that causes a stoppage of work.

  • Industrial Action:

    • The settling of a dispute about working conditions.

  • Non-wage Outcomes:

    • Benefits form working that are not related to wages.

  • Outsourcing:

    • Giving tasks to another firm to do.

  • Mobility of Labour:

    • Ease where labour can be moved from one geographic location to another.

  • Participation Rate:

    • Percentage of working age population in labour force.

  • Part-time Employment:

    • Individual works less than (normal week) 35 hours a week.

  • Privatisation:

    • Selling off government businesses and assets to the private sector.

  • Productivity of Labour:

    • Output per worker, or output per hour worked.

  • Remuneration:

    • Payment for labour

  • Reservation Wage:

    • Minimum wage individual is willing to work for.

  • Strike:

    • A withdrawel of labour.

  • Structural Unemployment:

    • Unemployment that comes from a mismatch between skills demanded and skills supplied by workers.

  • Sub-contracting:

    • Contractor giving parts of their contracts to other contractors.

  • Trade Union:

    • Body that represents workers in an industry for their benefit.

  • Underemployment:

    • Workers that wish to work more hours.

  • Underutilisation Rate:

    • Unemployment rate and underemployment rate added together.

  • Unemployment:

    • In work force, not employed, actively seeking and available for work.
