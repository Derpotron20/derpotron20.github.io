• Advertising:

    • Informing consumers about product to increase sales.

  • Allocative Efficiency:

    • Benefit to society is maximised.

  • Autonomous Consumption (a):

    • Amount person will spend when Y = 0.

  • Average Cost:

    • Cost per unit at a given level of output.

  • Complimentary Goods:

    • Good where price of one and demand for another are inversely related, e.g. printers & ink.

  • Consumption (C):

    • Part of disposable Y spent on G/S.

  • Consumption Function:

    • Equation of relationship between Y & C.

  • Costs:

    • Payments made by firms to attract resources.

  • Deseconomies of Scale:

    • Increases in LRAC from increase in scale of operations.

  • Dissaving:

    • When C > Y, so S must be used.

  • External Economies of Scale:

    • Benefits to a firm from external factors independant to the firm.

  • Fixed Cost:

    • Does not change with output, e.g. factory, interest.

  • Globalisation:

    • International nature of markets.

  • Gross Income:

    • Total Y before deductions.

  • Household Savings Ratio:

    • Ratio of S to Y.

  • Industry:

    • Group of firms producing very similar products.

  • Interest:

    • Price paid for borrowed money & K, % yield of money.

  • Internal Economies of Scale:

    • Action by firm to reduce average costs, usually from specialisation.

  • Life-cycle Hypothesis of Saving:

    • > Y leads to > S, same vice versa.

  • Market:

    • Situation where buyers and sellers interact to exchange G/S.

  • Net Income:

    • Gross Y - costs.

  • Productive Capacity:

    • Total production possible when using all resources efficiently.

  • Productive Efficiency:

    • Level of output is minimum average total cost.

  • Profit:

    • Reward for entrepreneurship. Payment for uncertainty and risk.

  • Rent:

    • Payment for use of land and natural resources.

  • Satisficing:

    • The pursuit of a firm to meet a combination of it's objectives.

  • Substitute Goods:

    • Demand for one good and price of another are directly related.

  • Transfer Payments:

    • Payments form government to household

  • Utility:

    • Level of satisfaction gained from consuming a product.

  • Variable Cost:

    • Expenses of firms that change with output.

  • Wages:

    • Price paid for labour.
