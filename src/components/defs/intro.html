• Business Cycle:

    • Cyclical business fluctuations (boom, trough, recession contraction).

  • Capital Goods (K):

    • A good or service that produces another good or service.

  • Circular Flow of Income:

    • Model that describes flow of money, good, & services between major sectors of the economy.

  • Consumer Goods (C):

    • For consumers satisfaction.

  • Consumer Sovereignty:

    • Consumer decides "what".

  • Demand:

    • Quantity a consumer is willing to buy at a certain price. Only if definite plan to & able to purchase.

  • Economic Growth:

    • Increase in nations output (measured in GDP).

  • Enterprise:

    • Entrepreneur risk innovation.

  • Exports (X):

    • Goods sold to other countries.

  • Factors of Production:

    • The different resources used in production; Capital, labour, land, entrepreneurship.

  • Fair Work Comission:

    • Manages industrial relations.

  • Financial Sector:

    • Gets funds from savings & lend to firms for investment.

  • Firms Sector:

    • Businesses who employ resources, produce & sell goods and services.

  • Government Expenditure (G):

    • Money spent by government.

  • Government Sector:

    • Raises money through T, spends through G.

  • Gross Domestic Product (GDP):

    • Market value of all final goods and services. Y=C+I+G+(X-M)

  • Gross National Income (GNI):

    • GDP + receipts from other counters - income payments to other nations.

  • Household Sector:

    • Consumers, supply labour, purchase goods and services.

  • Imports (M):

    • Produced overseas for our use.

  • Industrial Relations:

    • Relationship between employees & employers.

  • Injections (IGX):

    • I + G + X

  • Investment:

    • Purchasing of capital.

  • Labour (L):

    • Mental or mannual effort.

  • Labour Force:

    • 15+ people who are employed or unemployed.

  • Land:

    • Natural resources used in production.

  • Oppurtunity Cost:

    • Cost of alternative forgone.

  • Overseas Sector:

    • Other economics that we export to or we import from.

  • Production Possibility Frontier (Curve):

    • Model of maximum production.

  • Productivity:

    • Quantity of output per worker

  • Quality of Life:

    • Overall standard of living & wellbeing.

  • Recession:

    • Two consecutive quarters of negative economic growth.

  • Resources:

    • Anything that can be used for production.

  • Savings (S):

    • Part of disposable Y not used for current consumption.

  • Scarcity:

    • Shortage of something that is wanted.

  • Taxation (T):

    • Main source of government Revenue.

  • Technological Change:

    • New & improved products.

  • Undermployemnet:

    • Desire additional hours of work.

  • Wants:

    • Desires for goods and services that satisfy.

  • Welfare:

    • Government repayment without providing something in return.

  • Withdrawels (STM):

    • S + T + M
